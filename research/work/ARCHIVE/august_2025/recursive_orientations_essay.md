## The Symmetry Trap

But why exactly three dimensions? Why not two, or four, or seventeen? The answer lies in a profound insight about symmetry and void collapse.

**Even-numbered dimensions create perfect symmetry, which is another form of the impossible void.**

**Three dimensions is the minimum asymmetry required for stable existence.**

With three orthogonal axes, you cannot achieve perfect symmetry. There's always some "leftover" asymmetry that prevents total cancellation.
