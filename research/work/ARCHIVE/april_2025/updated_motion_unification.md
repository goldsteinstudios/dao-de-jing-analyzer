**When Applied:** Systems where circular motion is directly observable
**Examples:**

- Electron orbitals around nuclei
- Vortex patterns in fluids
- Gyroscopic systems

**Mathematical Signature:**
```
r(θ) = orbital radius
ω = angular velocity
E = ½Iω² (rotational energy)
```

### **The Bellows (橐籥) - Linear Motion Serving Circulation**
**When Applied:** Systems where linear/oscillatory motion serves circular functions
**Examples:**
- Piston bellows creating pressure circulation
- Heart valves enabling blood circulation
- AC generators (rotation → oscillation → electrical circulation)
- Breathing patterns serving metabolic circulation

**Mathematical Signature:**
```
x(t) = A cos(ωt + φ) (linear motion)
Serves: Circular energy, pressure, or material flows
Same ω appears in both linear component and system circulation
```

### **The Vessel (器) - Wave Motion in Circulation Systems**
**When Applied:** Wave patterns that emerge from or serve circulation
**Examples:**
- Ocean waves (circulation cells in water)
- Sound waves (pressure circulation through media)
- Electromagnetic waves (field circulation patterns)
- Information waves through networks

**Mathematical Signature:**
```
Ψ(x,t) = A cos(kx - ωt + φ)
Wavelength λ = spatial period of circulation pattern
Frequency f = circulation rate of underlying system
```
