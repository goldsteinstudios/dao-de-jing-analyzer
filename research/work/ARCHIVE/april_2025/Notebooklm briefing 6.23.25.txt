Briefing: The Structural Recursion Model of Reality
This briefing summarizes a novel structural recursion model of reality, focusing on its core axioms, definitions, and implications across various scientific domains. The model posits that reality is fundamentally structured by the interplay of gradients, paradoxes, and recursive turning, rather than being composed of discrete "things."
I. Foundational Axioms and Structural Conditions
The model begins with pre-axiomatic structural conditions that define the nature of reality:
	•	Reality is Infinitely Vast and Infinitely Divisible: There is "no smallest scale, and no largest extent." Reality cannot be "finished"; it is not built from finite units or discrete parts, but from "recursive distinctions." If finite, recursion would halt. 	•	The Void (P₀): Undivided Potential: The "Void" is not emptiness or nothingness, but the "unstructured condition from which paradox arises." It is the absence of distinction, "pure undivided potential," where "no difference to be found," and "no quality to be measured." This condition is inherently unstable, as "indistinction cannot exclude contrast without implying it." ("Our_Infinite_Reality_Draft_5.1.25.pdf", "Part_1_4.28.25.pdf")

	1.	Axiom 1: All Qualities Exist Only in Contrast (X₀/Xn): "No value (hot, cold, light, dark, motion, stillness) can be defined except as part of a structural gradient."
