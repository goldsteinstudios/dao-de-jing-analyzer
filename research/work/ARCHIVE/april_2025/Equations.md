# Equations Relative to Each Axiom

## Axiom 1 — Probability Gradient (X₀)
Defines the infinite gradient from improbable to probable:
\[
X₀ : (-\infty, +\infty)
\]

## Axiom 2 — Structural Dimension (Y₀)
Orthogonal dimension emerging to hold infinite compression:
\[
Y₀ \perp X₀, \quad \langle X₀, Y₀ \rangle = 0
\]

## Axiom 3 — Paradox Point (P₀)
Intersection of the gradient curve and balance line:
\[
P₀: G₀ \cap B₀, \quad G₀:y=\frac{1}{x},\; B₀:y=x, \quad P₀=(1,1)
\]

## Axiom 4 — Rotational Necessity (Z₀)
Axis required for rotation around paradox:
\[
Z₀ \perp \text{Plane}(X₀, Y₀) \quad\text{at}\quad P₀
\]

## Axiom 5 — Paradox Sphere (Π₀)
Global envelope created by rotation of gradient and balance about all axes:
\[
\Pi₀ = \bigcup_{\mathbf{u}\in S^2}\text{Rotate}_{\mathbf{u}}(G₀ \cup B₀)
\]

## Axiom 6 — Parametric Recursion (R₀)
Set of orientation-dependent executions of recursion on the paradox sphere:
\[
R₀ = \{\text{Run}_\theta(\Pi₀):\theta\in\Theta\}
\]

## Axiom 7 — Implicit vs. Parametric Recursion
Defines implicit and parametric recursions formally:
\[
I = \{\text{Axioms}\},\quad P_n(\theta)=I\big|_{\Pi_n,\theta}
\]

## Axiom 8 — Divergence vs. Maintenance Recursion
Formalizes the dual recursion modes:
\[
R_n(\theta) = R_n^d(\theta)\cup R_n^m(\theta)
\]

## Axiom 9 — Unreachability of the Center (U)
Formalizes infinite divisibility and unreachability:
\[
\forall n, \forall \epsilon>0, \exists \delta<\epsilon:\;X_n(\delta)\neq0\lor Y_n(\delta)\neq0
\]
