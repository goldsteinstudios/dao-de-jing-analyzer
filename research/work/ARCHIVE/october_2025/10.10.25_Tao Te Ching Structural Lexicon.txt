## 4 · Structural Gloss of Chapter 1 (Tao ke Tao)

> 道可道，非常道。名可名，非常名。無名天地之始；有名萬物之母。  
> 故常無欲以觀其妙；常有欲以觀其徼。此兩者同出而異名，同謂之玄。玄之又玄，眾妙之門。

| Line | Structural Reading |
|------|--------------------|
| 道可道 | Reality can manifest in parametric forms (O₁ → P₁). |
| 非常道 | But no manifest form captures the implicit whole (O₁). |
| 名可名 | Distinction can occur (M operator active). |
| 非常名 | But constant naming would destroy contrast (balance lost). |
| 無名天地之始 | Named-nothing is the origin (O₁) of Heaven/Earth gradient (Y₁/X₁). |
| 有名萬物之母 | Named-something (P₁) generates the ten-thousand manifest forms (Rₙ). |
| 此兩者同出而異名 | These two co-emerge and are distinguished only by orientation. |
| 同謂之玄 | Their unity is the mysterious paradox (P₀ preserved). |
| 玄之又玄 | Recursion of mystery — P₀ → O₁ → P₁ → O₂ … |
| 眾妙之門 | The gateway (門) to all emergence — recursive circulation of forms. |

## 5 · Summary Logic

1. **Míng 可 Míng 非 常 Míng** — Naming is the condition for contrast.  
2. **Wú / Yǒu co-emerge** — Void and form exist as a single gradient.  
3. **Dào → O₁ ** — Origin frame arises by rotating paradox.  
4. **Fǎn 者 Dào 之 動** — Return is the movement of Tao: recursion.  
5. **Shēng 者 萬 物 之 宗** — Generation through recursion creates all forms.
