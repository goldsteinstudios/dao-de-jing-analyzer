## What the Smith Actually Does

Before quenching, the smith applies clay in a pattern:
- Thick clay on the spine
- Thin or no clay on the edge
- Gradient between

Then the whole blade goes into water.

### What Happens

| Zone | Clay Coverage | Cooling Rate | Crystal Structure | Result |
|------|---------------|--------------|-------------------|--------|
| Edge | None/thin | Rapid (淬) | Locked high-energy | 剛 (hard, holds edge, brittle) |
| Spine | Thick | Slow (退) | Relaxed equilibrium | 柔 (flexible, absorbs shock) |
| Boundary | Gradient | Variable | Transitional | The hamon (visible interface) |

The smith doesn't choose 剛 or 柔. The smith chooses *where each one goes*.

## Why This Changes Chapter 76

### Traditional Reading

> 堅強者死之徒，柔弱者生之徒
> "The hard and stiff are death's followers; the soft and yielding are life's followers."

Sounds like moral advice: be gentle, not rigid.

### What the Smith Knows

A blade that's *entirely* 柔 won't cut. It bends. Useless.
A blade that's *entirely* 剛 shatters on first impact. Useless.

The functional blade has **both properties in correct distribution**:
- 剛 at the edge (where cutting happens)
- 柔 at the spine (where shock absorption happens)
- Gradient between (where forces transition)

### The Correct Reading

Chapter 76 isn't saying "be soft." Chapter 76 documents what the smith already knows:

**Pure 剛 fails. Pure 柔 fails. Correct distribution succeeds.**
